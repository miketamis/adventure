GJELI QË PIELL FLORI, PULA QË PIELL GJARPËRINJ — "The Rooster who Lays Gold,
the Hen who Lays Serpents" (Dozon 1879, chrestomathie tale VIII). NOT the
original of Elsie's "Half Rooster" (FOLKLORE id: half-rooster, Elsie folktale
15) — logged here only as a related variant found while re-hunting that tale's
Albanian original; see half-rooster.js's albanian.variants for how it differs.

Source:  Auguste Dozon, Manuel de la langue chkipe ou albanaise. Grammaire,
         vocabulaire, chrestomathie (Paris: Ernest Leroux, 1879),
         chrestomathie tale VIII, "Le coq qui pond de l'or et la poule qui
         pond des serpents", pp. 38-39.
Extract: raw OCR lines 1581-1618 of the local file
         docs/references/dozon-manuel-langue-chkipe.fr-sq.txt, 2026-07-15.
Notes:   Dozon prints no Albanian title (French title only, given above in
         his own orthography). His transcription is French-based: oe-ligature
         (œ)=ë, ou=u, y=j (gy=gj, ky=q), ç=sh, tç=ç, lh=ll. This is a FULL
         running Albanian narrative, not a table-of-contents line or a bare
         dictionary example — a poor man's rooster gorges on the king's gold,
         feigns death to be thrown out, then tells the old man to hang it
         head-down and beat it with a stick so gold pours from its beak; the
         envious old woman's hen, coached by the rooster to eat a serpent
         instead of learning the real trick, is hung up the same way and
         disgorges live serpents that devour the old woman. It shares the
         SHAPE of Elsie's ending (a bird that yields gold under a daily
         beating; an envious neighbour's copy-cat animal that ends in
         disaster) but none of the specifics: no half-a-bird split, no
         belly-swallowed companions (frog/fox/wolf/mouse), no cabbage-garden
         capture, no oven/stable/geese/chest trials, and the punishment
         itself differs (hung head-down and beaten vs. beaten across the
         back; the old woman is devoured by serpents vs. she kills the cat
         and dies of her own rage). Because the specific events diverge, this
         text cannot be spliced in as the verbatim Albanian for half-rooster's
         beats 8-10 without misrepresenting what it actually says.
