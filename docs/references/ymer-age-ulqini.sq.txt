Ymer Agë Ulqini
================
The sung ballad (kângë) of Aga Ymer of Ulqin/Ulcinj — the "returning husband"
epic behind the FOLKLORE entry `aga-ymer`. Wikibooks source template read as
{{libri|title|place recorded|collector|year}}: title "Ymer Agë Ulqini",
recorded in Arbnesh/Krajë (the Albanian-speaking coast around Ulcinj,
Montenegro), collector Hipi Zhdripi, 2006.

Source: sq.wikibooks.org/wiki/Ymer_Agë_Ulqini (CC-BY-SA); raw wikitext incl.
a <pre> verse block, extracted 2026-07-15. This is NOT the same Albanian text
Mitrush Kuteli's prose (the one Robert Elsie translated into English as the
"Aga Ymeri of Ulqin" legend) was drawn from — Kuteli's own Albanian original
is not present in any local corpus — but it is a genuine, independently
collected Albanian telling of the SAME tale-type and title figure (captured
at war, a wife's sworn nine-years'-and-nine-days' wait, a disguised
homecoming, recognition by a bodily mark, and a kept besa that rides back to
prison and wins final freedom). See aga-ymer.js's `discrepancies` for exactly
where the two tellings diverge (years counted, ransom price, the recognition
token, the missing mother-at-the-fountain scene, the new groom's name).

The OCR/wikitext source is noisy (stray hyphens, "0"→"o", "T"/"l" glyph
confusion, e.g. "lmer"→"Imer", "rnreti"→"mretit"). Beat lines quote a
LIGHTLY CLEANED version (obvious scan-noise fixed only). The ballad's own
distinctive Gheg words — "agja", "erdh", "izën", "mret/mreti/mretit" (its
word for king; never "mbret"), "burk" (its word for prison; "burg" only in
the loanword "burgaxhi"), "rabazan" (its word for a captive; never "rob"),
the Gheg "-ue"/apocopated participles (shkue, martue, dek, hi, pa — never
the Tosk shkuar/martuar/dekur/hyrë/parë), the vocative "-o" — are kept
verbatim, not standardized to Tosk.

CORRECTION (2026-07-15): the hero's name is spelled "Ymer" only in the
collector's own title line and in this file's own header; inside the sung
verses themselves it is spelled "Imer"/"Imeri" almost without exception
(30+ times, incl. the corrupted first line "Aga Tmeri", resolved here to
"Imeri" for consistency with every other spelling in the verse body, not
to the title's "Ymeri"). An earlier pass through the beat lines in
../../src/game/data/tales/kostandini-i-vogel.js quietly standardized this
name AND dozens of other Gheg forms (mret→mbret, burk→burg, and the whole
Gheg "-ue" participle family →Tosk "-uar": shkue→shkuar, martue→martuar,
qerrue→qerruar, rranue→rrënuar, dek→dekur, hi→hyrë, etc.) to standard/Tosk
Albanian without disclosing it, so the quoted "Albanian original" did not
actually match this saved text. The beat lines have been corrected back to
this file's own Gheg spelling throughout (see that file's `albanian.source`
note); a handful of genuinely illegible OCR spans are now quoted as printed
with a bracketed [sic] rather than smoothed into confident modern Albanian.
The raw wikitext follows unedited below.
