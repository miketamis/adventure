¶1
[1.1] As might be expected, Djakova revels in the supernatural, and
miraculous happenings are frequent.
[1.2] In a mountain-side hard by, on the left of the road to Prizren, is
a magic cavern.
[1.3] For miles does it go underground; none knows how far, some say
even beneath the Drin.
[1.4] In it is a large and ancient city where no man now lives; but the
bazar, to this very day, is stocked with all that is finest and best —
fruit, flesh, fish, jewels, and fair raiment.
[1.5] But should any man venture to touch one single thing, his torch at
once goes out, serpents spring up and devour him in the darkness.
[1.6] And these are no serpents; they are oras (spirits) that guard the
cavern.
[1.7] No man has ventured in for many years.

¶2
[2.1] I said I would, and asked to be guided to the spot, but none dared
take me.
[2.2] Nor is this the sole spot that is miraculously guarded.

¶3
[3.1] Not far from Dyjakova, on a hill, are the ruins of a chapel.
[3.2] A Moslem tried to dig there for treasure, but was at once struck
dead by lightning out of a cloudless sky.
[3.3] Not sufficiently warned, some men went to remove stones for
building purposes, but a crowd of serpents at once leapt from the
ground, and the intruders only just escaped.
[3.4] For oras can take what form they please — birds, beasts, women, or
serpents.

¶4
[4.1] Quite recently a man was driving past with an oxcart, when both the
oxen fell on their knees before the ruins; and the holiness of the spot
being now proved beyond all doubt, none dare meddle with it in future.
