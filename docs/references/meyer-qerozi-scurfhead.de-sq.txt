Qerozi (Meyer's "The Scurfhead" parallel) — RAW, UNUSABLE OCR
================================================================
Candidate Albanian original for scurfhead (= Elsie's "The Scurfhead",
Albanian Folktales and Legends tale 2). This is the file src/game/data/
tales/scurfhead.js's `albanian.local` points to, per the tale schema's
"keep the RAW text in the local reference file" rule — but no beat
line's Albanian third element is quoted FROM this raw OCR (it is
unrecoverable letter-for-letter, see below); those lines were checked
word-for-word against the CLEAN page-image reconstruction instead, at
the sibling file docs/references/meyer-qerozi-scurfhead.sq.txt. This
raw file exists as the evidence trail for that reconstruction and for
a future pass by anyone with the original page images in hand.

Source: Gustav Meyer, Kurzgefasste albanesische Grammatik mit Lesestücken
und Glossar (Leipzig: Karl J. Trübner, 1888), Lesestücke §3 "Qerozi",
pp. 60–67. Same underlying folk tale as Thimi Mitko's Bleta shqypëtare
(Alexandria, 1878) collection that also gave Elsie tale 3 (three-friends);
Meyer's chrestomathy transcribes it in his own ad-hoc 1880s phonetic
alphabet for language learners, NOT standard Albanian orthography.

Digitisation: Google Books scan, mirrored at archive.org under TWO
identifiers that are the same underlying scan (confirmed identical OCR
text, checked 2026-07-14/15):
  - https://archive.org/details/kurzgefasstealb00meyegoog
  - https://archive.org/details/bub_gb_brfk0NiwGZMC
No cleaner alternate scan or transcription was found.

RAW OCR — uncorrected, exactly as extracted from the djvu.txt fulltext
(the local copy already on disk at
docs/references/meyer-kurzgefasste-grammatik.de-sq.txt, lines 4984-5309).
Extracted 2026-07-15.

WHY THIS ISN'T QUOTED IN THE GAME: Meyer's phonetic alphabet plus the
scan's OCR corruption compound into wording that cannot be recovered
letter-for-letter with confidence — e.g. line 7 below, "^e he mberet ke
kis tre diema, kih eöe he köpehte te bükure fort", almost certainly
renders "e një mbret që kishte tre djemë, kishte edhe një kopsht të
bukur fort" ("and a king who had three sons, he also had a very
beautiful garden") given the parallel English, but a full sentence-by-
sentence reconstruction at this confidence level across ~90 lines would
mean composing modern Albanian wording from plot-context guesses, not
quoting the original — which the tale schema explicitly forbids
("never invent, paraphrase, or back-translate Albanian").
