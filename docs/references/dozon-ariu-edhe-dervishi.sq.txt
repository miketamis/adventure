ARIU EDHE DERVISHI — the Albanian original of "The Bear and the Dervish"
(Elsie's folktale 12; FOLKLORE id: bear-dervish)

Source:  Auguste Dozon, Manuel de la langue chkipe ou albanaise. Grammaire,
         vocabulaire, chrestomathie (Paris: Ernest Leroux, 1879),
         chrestomathie tale III, "L'ours et le derviche", pp. 25-28.
         (Reprinted in Folklor shqiptar 1, Proza popullore, Tirana 1963 —
         the text Elsie translated.)
Extract: raw OCR lines 1001-1151 of the local file
         docs/references/dozon-manuel-langue-chkipe.fr-sq.txt, 2026-07-14.
Notes:   Dozon prints no Albanian title (French title only). His transcription
         is French-based: oe-ligature=ë, ou=u, u=y, y=j (gy=gj, ky=q, ny=nj),
         ç=sh, tç=ç, lh=ll, rh=rr, ts=c, dj=xh, dz=z/x. The beat lines in
         src/game/data/tales/bear-dervish.js transliterate this text to modern
         orthography with light OCR repair (e.g. "Kvè"=Qe, "Hrét"=Pret,
         "nag"=nga, "iï/ñ"=nj as in "n'a du plyéçta"=nja dy pleshta,
         "odzler"=nxjerrë, "Wanisnœ"=llanisnë, "clôt"=dot, "(16"=dô=do;
         one OCR sentence break re-seated: "edhé vâte pœr droû. Ariou me te
         vâtour mœ nœ ergelyé rhœmbéou nœ kâ" — the dervish goes for wood,
         the bear seizes the ox, as Elsie also reads it).
         "çékouy"=shekuj: Dozon's own vocabulary glosses "Çékye, seau à
         traire" (milking pail) — «tre shekuj me gjizë» = three pails of curd.
